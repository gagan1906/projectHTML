Triumph Group, Inc. is a supplier of aerospace services, structures, systems and support. Based in Berwyn, Pennsylvania, United States, Triumph engineers, designs, and manufactures aircraft components, systems and accessories. Several services and products are offered through three of their operating organizations, Integrated Systems, Aerospace Structures, and Product Support

Founding Years (1885-1902):
The origins of Triumph can be traced back to 1885 when a German immigrant named Siegfried Bettmann founded S. Bettmann & Co. in London. Initially, the company imported bicycles from Europe. In 1887, Moritz Schulte joined Bettmann, and the company name was changed to Bettmann & Schulte. The first Triumph-branded bicycles were produced in 1889.
Motorcycle Production (1902-1914):
Triumph entered the motorcycle market in 1902 with the introduction of its first motorcycle. The company gained prominence for its innovation and engineering excellence. By 1910, Triumph was one of the largest motorcycle manufacturers in the world.
World War I (1914-1918):
During World War I, Triumph played a significant role by producing motorcycles for the British military. The Model H, introduced in 1915, became widely used by the British Army.
Interwar Period (1919-1939):
After the war, Triumph continued to thrive and introduced new models. In 1936, Edward Turner joined Triumph, marking the beginning of a new era for the company. Turner's designs would go on to shape Triumph motorcycles for decades.
World War II (1939-1945):
Once again, Triumph contributed to the war effort by producing motorcycles for the military. The company's factory in Coventry suffered significant damage during German bombing raids.
Post-War Period (1945-1960s):
Triumph experienced a post-war resurgence with the introduction of the Speed Twin in 1937 and later the Thunderbird in 1949. These models helped establish Triumph as a leading motorcycle manufacturer.
